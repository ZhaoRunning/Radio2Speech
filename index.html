Radio2Speech: High Quality Speech Recovery from Radio Frequency Signals

Running Zhao1,3             Jiangtao Yu2,3             Tingle Li2,3
Hang Zhao2,3             Edith C.H. Ngai1
1The University of Hong Kong        2 IIIS, Tsinghua University         3Shanghai Qi Zhi Institute


We introduce Radio2Speech, a system that uses radio frequency (RF) signals to recover speech of corpus with high quality. It transmits the mmWave frequency-modulated continuous wave (FMCW) signal to the loudspeaker, and parses the reflected signal for recovery. Radio2Speech can recover speech comparable to the quality of a microphone, advancing from recovering only single tone music or incomprehensible speech in existing approaches.
In this webpage, we show some reference speeches and recovered speeches of our proposed Radio2Speech and WaveEar[1]. In quiet scenario, Radio2Speech can recover high quality speech like a microphone. Since RF signals are not affected by the noise in noisy scenario, our system is still able to recover high quality speech, while microphone almost fails. The existence of soundproof glass can make the microphone deaf, but it does nothing to RF signals. Thus, the speech recovered by Radio2Speech remains intelligible.
